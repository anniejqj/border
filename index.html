Gendered Friction

Women's Invisible Labor Along the China–Vietnam Border

This study examines how Vietnamese women at the Dongxing–Móng Cái borderland absorb the institutional costs of cross-border mobility through invisible forms of labor—administrative, reproductive, emotional, and risk-related. The concept of "gendered friction" reveals how border governance functions at low cost by transferring its burdens onto women's daily practices, making their labor essential yet systematically unrecognized.
